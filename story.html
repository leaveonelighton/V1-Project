A Story in the Light

The Door They Opened

One ordinary act of compassion changed my life.

When I was thirteen years old, I arrived at 5 Cedar Hill Road frightened and searching for somewhere to belong.

Peg and Jerry Gorman did not make grand promises. They simply opened their door, made room at their table, and treated me like I mattered.

They could not erase everything that had happened to me. But they noticed me—and they acted.

That is compassion.

Compassion does not require us to solve every problem. That is God's work. Compassion asks us to notice the person in front of us and do the next kind thing we are able to do.

God used one family's open door to change my life. Their simple act of compassion continues to inspire me today.

Who might need you to notice them?
